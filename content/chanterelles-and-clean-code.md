Last fall I spent a morning foraging for chanterelles in a patch of old-growth forest. I came home with a basket of golden mushrooms and a nagging thought about refactoring.

Both activities share a strange rhythm: you scan, you recognize a pattern, you decide whether to act. And in both cases, the biggest mistake is not knowing when to stop.

## Pattern recognition

When you're looking for chanterelles, you learn to see the forest differently. The color of the soil matters. The type of trees. The way light falls on the mossy ground. After a while, your brain starts pattern-matching before your conscious mind catches up.

Refactoring code is the same. You develop an eye for duplication, for awkward abstractions, for functions that are doing too much. The patterns reveal themselves once you've trained your attention.

Know when to put the basket down.
